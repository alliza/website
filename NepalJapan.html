Japan and Nepal

Published on sep 06,2015

Geographical comparision

The island nation of East Asia located in the Pacific Ocean is officially called the Nippon Koku, which means State of Japan. The neighboring countries of North Korea, South Korea, Russia and China are located west of Japan. The islands of Japan lies east of Sea of Japan, and the East China Sea is situated to its south and the Philippine Sea to its southeast. Japan comprises 6852 islands, Honshu, Kyushu, Shikoku and Hokkaido are the largest and prominent islands of Japan. In fact, about 80% of Japan’s population lives on Honshu Island. The islands of Japan lies between 24° and 46°N Latitudes and 122° and 146°E longitudes covering a total area of 377,955 sq. km. Japan is the sixty second largest country of the world. It has a coastline of 29, 751 km long with no land boundary. Japan lies in the Pacific Ring of Fire, it has 108 active volcanoes and is highly prone to volcanoes, Tsunamis and earthquakes. Tokyo the capital city of Japan is one of the prominent economic centers of the world. In fact, economically Japan is one of the world’s strongest economies. Climatically Japan has largely temperate climate.
       The country Nepal is in the Asia continent and the longitude and latitude of this country are 27.9389° N9408° E, 84. It lies in between India and China. It is landlock country.

Cultural comparision

Both countries have influenced with Buddhism.But Nepal is famous for hindu country.Nepal is equally famous for birthplace of Gautam Buddha. The Buddhist monks and monasteries have attracted the Japanese from the very early days.The people of Japan have a deep respect to their monarch especially after what they achieved from Emperor Meiji in 1869
Besides Japanese language has become very popular in Nepal. Many Japanese language institutes have been opened at the initiation of the individuals as well as the government. A foreign language campus under the Tribhuvan University started its Japanese language class right from 1965. It also helping the tightening the cultural relationship between two countries.

Economy comparision

Nepal is amonng the least developed countries in the world.Nepal is heavily dependent on remittances, which amount to as much as 22-25% of GDP. Agriculture is the mainstay of the economy, providing a livelihood for more than 70% of the population and accounting for a little over one-third of GDP. Industrial activity mainly involves the processing of agricultural products, including pulses, jute, sugarcane, tobacco, and grain.etc Japan is the richest country in the world.It develop a technologically advanced economy.Though the economy has fallen into recession three times since 2008.The new government will continue a longstanding debate on restructuring the economy and reining in Japan's huge government debt, which is exceeding 230% of GDP. To help raise government revenue and reduce public debt, Japan decided in 2013 to gradually increase the consumption tax to a total of 10% by the year 2015. Simillarly Nepal lack sufficient capital to invest in industries and other development efforts. It needs to attract the foreign direct investment (FDI) from the donor countries to speed up the process of industrialization and export promotion. Japan is already in third position in the field of FDI in Nepal after India and the United States with at least 44 Japanese private investments running.

Conclusion

The cultural, economic relations between Nepal and Japan have shown that the bilateral relations between these two countries are based on mutual understanding, friendship and cooperation. Buddhism and the Buddhist tradition of Nepal have become the source of spirituality to the Japanese people and Japan's drive for modernization has become the source of inspiration to the people of Nepal. Now Japan is the major development partner of Nepal that has contributed in promoting Nepal's national security through various schemes and projects. Japan's help in the development of infrastructure to boost the economic development; education, health and sanitation; democracy building; cultural exchanges; water resources; food security; foreign direct investment; global and regional integration of the Nepali economy
